Ramen

Ramen is a popular Japanese noodle soup consisting of wheat noodles served in a flavorful broth, which can be soy sauce, miso, or pork-based. The dish is typically garnished with toppings like sliced pork (chashu), soft-boiled eggs, nori (seaweed), green onions, and bamboo shoots.

    Ingredients

    4 oz. fresh ramen noodles

    1 1/2 c. chicken dashi

    2 reserved shiitake mushrooms, sliced

    3 Tbsp. or more tare, to taste

    2 slices chashu

    1 nitamago, halved

    1 green onion, white and light green parts only, very thinly sliced

    2 tsp. la-yu

    Nori (optional).


Directions

  1. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, bring chicken broth to a bare simmer. Remove from heat and let cool 2 minutes. Add in mushrooms and kombu (kelp) and let steep 5 minutes. Then, add in bonito and let steep 5 more minutes. Strain and save solids for nitamago process and serving.

  2. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, bring all ingredients except pork to a low simmer and reduce heat to low.

  3. Meanwhile, in another medium pot over medium heat, bring pork and 6 cups water to a low simmer. Drain immediately and gently rinse pork.

  4. Place pork in tare pot and bring to a simmer. Cover and continue to cook on low until tender, turning pork occasionally, about 1 hour 20 minutes.

  5. Let cool for 20 minutes, then strain and reserve liquid for serving. Refrigerate pork and remaining liquid separately. Slice pork before serving, lightly torched, if desired.

  6. In a pot of salted boiling water, cook ramen, stirring with tongs or chopsticks until al dente, about 1 minute. (If using instant ramen, discard seasoning packet and follow packet instructions to cook until al dente.) Drain well.

  7. In a small saucepan over medium heat, warm dashi and shiitake until barely simmering. Cook for 1 minute and remove from heat. Set shiitake aside.

  8. Add dashi, tare, and noodles to serving bowl. Top with chashu, nitamago, shiitake, green onion, a drizzle of la-yu, and nori, if using.
